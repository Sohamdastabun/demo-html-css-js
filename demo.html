Botani

The Department was established in the year of 1994 with the initiation of Honours courses in Botany. Around 1953 onwards it was germinated as an Intermediate Streams (I.Sc) without introduction of Biological sciences and passing the days the stream was elevated to B.Sc (Pass course) with Botany, Zoology and Chemistry as combination subjects probably in the year of 1967. System has been changed and now the new pattern has been introduced as 1+1+1 systems in parallel with the Honours courses. The journey was at its very teenage as no full time lecturers at the beginning except a couple of fulltime teachers designated as Instructors. However, the college authority engaged two part-time teachers to run the department in very late stage. The gravity of the department is changing towards better direction. A good number of pass out students already have gone through better opportunity with white colour jobs, NET and RET qualified etc.

Computer Science

The Department of Computer Science and Applications was established in the academic year 2001-02 as a complete and separate self-financing unit under the tutelage of the same Principal and same Governing Body of Malda College. The G.B., Malda College has formed an Individual Committee viz. Monitoring Committee which used to take decision about the development of the department. This department was set-up predominantly to cater the demand of the local people, who want to study the recent technologies within an affordable fee. Primarily there was a single course offered by the department namely Bachelor of Computer Application (BCA). From 2003-04 a new course B. Sc. in Computer Science has been started as a honours course by the department to fulfil the desire of the people.

Chemistry

The Department of Chemistry was established in the academic year 1964. An eminent academician Prof. Biman Palit took the initiative to establish the department of Chemistry. It is first science department of Malda College and fulfilled the local as well as surrounding demand of the subject. Department of Chemistry is a well-equipped undergraduate department and each year more than 50% admitted masters students in Chemistry are from Malda college.
